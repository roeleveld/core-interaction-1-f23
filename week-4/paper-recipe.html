Oliebollen

Introduction

Every winter, as December approaches the new year, Dutch towns are full of the fried scent of Oliebollen.
Like a New Orleans beignet, these fried dough balls are made of a yeasted batter, but these are studded with chopped apples and raisins. The raisins caramelise in the hot oil, and the apples soften – a perfect backdrop for the avalanche of powdered sugar these are blanketed in.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet (7g) dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 apple, peeled and chopped
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Dissolve yeast in warm milk, add sugar, and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour and salt. Make a well in the center.
  3. Pour the yeast mixture into the well and stir until you have a smooth batter.
  4. Cover the bowl with a cloth and let the batter rise for 1 hour.
  5. Beat the eggs and fold them into the batter, followed by chopped apple pieces.
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C).
  7. Use two spoons to drop portions of the batter into the hot oil.
  8. Fry until golden brown, turning to cook evenly (about 5 minutes).
  9. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  10. Sprinkle with powdered sugar and enjoy!
